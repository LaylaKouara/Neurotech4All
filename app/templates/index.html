About

Our Mission

Neurotech for All explores how neurotechnology can be developed and implemented in ways that are fair, inclusive, and responsive to a wide range of people and contexts. The project focuses on identifying structural and technical factors that shape access and outcomes across different populations.

Our team brings together expertise across neuroscience, engineering, social science, and community-based research. We aim to inform how technologies are evaluated, tested, and applied so that they better reflect the diversity of those who may use them.

"We envision a future where neurotechnology is developed through approaches that recognise and respond to different experiences and needs."

Ethical Design

Exploring ways to support inclusive and responsible approaches to technology development.

Community Engagement

Working with communities to understand different perspectives and experiences of neurotechnology.

Innovative Research

Investigating how neurotechnologies perform across different user groups and contexts.

Supporting shared learning through resources and collaborative activities.

Research Themes

Our work is organised around three key areas:

Bias in Design and Performance

Investigating how hardware and software design can lead to unequal performance across different demographic groups.

  • Analysis of device calibration standards
  • Evaluation of physiological and anatomical variability
  • Computational modelling of performance differences
Learn more
Participation in Research

Understanding why some groups are underrepresented in neurotechnology research and exploring ways to address these gaps.

  • Community-led interviews and focus groups
  • Cultural and structural barriers to inclusion
  • Development of inclusive research practices
Learn more
Use and Access Disparities

Exploring patterns of underutilisation in clinical settings and identifying systemic barriers to accessing neurotechnology.

  • Review of health records and referral data
  • Interviews with patients and clinicians
  • Identification of social and institutional factors
